\begin{abstract}

Modern virtualization environments often face significant performance overheads when utilizing Virtual Input/Output Memory Management Units (vIOMMUs). This paper evaluates vFree, an optimized suite of page unmapping strategies designed to mitigate these bottlenecks. This work builds off of Fast \& Safe IO Memory Protection ~\cite{10.1145/3694715.3695943}. Our results demonstrate that for transmit (TX) flows, the vFree strategy achieves optimal performance, matching the aggregate throughput of systems with the vIOMMU disabled across configurations ranging from 1 to 16 VMs.  However, we identify a significant performance discrepancy in the receive (RX) flows, where the aggregate throughput decreases as a function of the number of VMs. This work provides a comparative analysis of paging strategies in cases with multiple VMs and highlights the remaining challenges.

\end{abstract}
